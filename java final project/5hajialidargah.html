Haji Ali Dargah, Mumbai

Iconic Mosque & Tomb by the Sea

About Haji Ali Dargah

Haji Ali Dargah is a mosque and tomb located on an islet off the coast of Worli in Mumbai. It was built in 1431 in memory of Sayed Peer Haji Ali Shah Bukhari, a wealthy merchant who renounced all worldly possessions before making a pilgrimage to Mecca. The mosque is accessible via a narrow causeway and attracts thousands of visitors every day.
